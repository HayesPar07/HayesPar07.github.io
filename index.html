Shrimp Ceviche

Ingredients

1 1/2 pounds peeled raw shrimp, diced Juice of five limes
2 tablespoons jalapeños, seeded and chopped
1/4 cup red onion, minced
1 Cucumber peeled, seeded and diced
2 Avocados, diced
1 bunch cilantro, chopped
Dash salt

Instructions

Rinse shrimp under cold water and pat dry. Place shrimp in a large zip-lock bag with lime juice, jalapeño, red onion, and salt. Toss and refrigerate one hour or until shrimpare opaque. When ready to serve toss in cucumber and avocado. Serve with tortilla chips.

Creamy Crab and Artichoke Dip
Ingredients

1 (8-ounce) cream cheese, room temperature
1 cup mayonnaise
1 package imitation crabmeat
1/3 chopped onion
1/3 can (13 3/4-ounce) artichoke hearts, drained and chopped
3/4 cup (3-ounces) shredded Parmesan cheese.

Instructions

Preheat oven to 375 degrees. In medium mixing bowl, blend together the cream cheese and mayonnaise until smooth. Stir in remaining ingredients and place mixture in glass pie plate. Bake uncovered for 15 to 18 minutes, until heated throughout and lightly brown. Serve with crackers or french bread.

Stuffed Mushrooms Ingredients

1 (8-ounce) package whole fresh mushrooms
2 tablespoons of butter
3-ounces of cream cheese, softened
1/3 cup Parmesan cheese
2 tablespoons milk
1 tablespoon of ground black pepper.

Instructions

Clean mushrooms and remove stems. Melt butter and roll mushrooms in melted butter. Mix softened cream cheese, Parmesan cheese, milk, and black pepper. Spoon cheese mixture into mushroom caps until even with surface of mushroom. Place in baking dish or foil lined cookie sheet with cheese side up in a 350 degree oven for 20 minutes or until cheese is slightly brown.

Bacon Wrapped Duck Appetizers Ingredients

4 duck breasts, skinned and seasoned with seasoned salt
1/2 onion, coarsley chopped
1/4 bell pepper, coarsley chopped
2 cups Italian salad dressing
Toothpicks soaked in water
1/4 pound Monterey Jack cheese, cubed Jalapeño slices
6 slices of bacon, cut into thirds

Instructions

Cut duck breasts into one-inch cubes. Season with seasoned salt. Place in plastic bag with onion, bell pepper, and Italian seaosning. Marinate overnight or for at least two hours in the refrigerator. Soak toothpicks in water. Take marinated duck breast cube, top with cube of Monterey Jack cheese, jalapeño slice, and wrap with bacon. Spear with toothpick. Grill over medium high fire for 10 to 15 minutes..

Cream of Broccoli Soup Ingredients

1 bunch broccoli
4 tablespoons butter
6 tablespoons flour
1 quart chicken stock, or 1 1/2 tablespoons chicken flavor base with 4 cups broccoli stock
Seasoned salt
Pepper to taste
1 small onion
1 cup whippin cream
Cheddar Cheese

Instructions

Cook broccoli in boiling water until soft. Drain, reservinig 4 cups broccoli stock for later, and set aside. Melt butter in another saucepan. Add flour. Stir roux until blended and cook 3 minutes. Add chicken stock or broccoli stock with chicken flavor base. Slowly add broth to roux. Cook until smooth and thick. Add broccoli and simmer about 30 minutes. Remove from heat and cool. Ladle soup into blender and blend until smooth. Pour into pot and add whipping cream. Heat but do not boil. Serve and top each bowl with grated cheese.

Chicken and Sausage Gumbo Ingredients

4 split breasts of chicken with skin
2 cups chopped seasoning(onion, bell pepper, celery, and garlic)
3 tablespoons cooking oil
1 package gumbo mix
1 pound cooked smoked sausage

Instructions

In 2 quarts of water boil chicken breasts for 45 minutes. Season well with salt, pepper, cajun seasoning, and garlic salt. Remove chicken and de-bone breast meat. Save the chicken stock. Sauté chopped seasonings in oil until wilted. Add chicken stock. Stir in package of gumbo mix. Cook over medium heat until completely dissolved. Add chicken breast meat and sausage, sliced. Salt and pepper to taste. Lower heat and simmer for 45 minutes. Serve over steamed rice.

Poke Chop Casserole Ingredients

2 tablespoons cooking oil
6 to 8 pork chops
2 (10 3/4-ounce) cans beef broth
2 cups rice, uncooked
1 small to medium onion, chopped
1 small to medium bell pepper, chopped
2 teaspoons garlic and herb seasoning blend
1 tablespoon parsley flakes, optional seasoned salt to taste

Instructions

Place oil in skillet. Season pork chops with seasoned salt. Precook pork chops, just until browned. Set aside. Combine remaining ingredients in a 9x13 baking pan. Stir to blend. Place pork chops on top of mixture. Cover with foil and bake 45 minutes to 1 hour at 350 degrees.
